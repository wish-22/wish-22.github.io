Orbsort is an old game I made while I was in high school. I still look back on it fondly for being my first full release and still being high quality. I did all the work; gameplay, graphics, audio.

In Orbsort, your goal is to sort colored circles in groups of four. You can take as long as you like in the standard Infinite mode, but you get more points if you can make "combos" (capsules sorted in rapid succession).

Nonononogram is a small game I made for a friend that operates along the same lines as Orbsort. It's more stripped back; where Orbsort has multiple modes, Nonononogram is simply just the Infinite mode.

Bulletlop is a bullet hell shoot-em-up arcade game me and two friends made for the GMTK game jam, a 4-day game development competition.

The main gimmick is that your gameplay is recorded; then, on the next loop, your gameplay is played back to you as an enemy.

We managed to get within the top 3% against 9,588 games for both categories "Enjoyment" and "Audio", though it was my friends NathanDAWs and Robyn Starre who did the music
